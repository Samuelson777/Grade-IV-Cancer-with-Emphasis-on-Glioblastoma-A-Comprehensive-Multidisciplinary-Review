## 📄 Overview

This repository contains a **comprehensive research review** on **Grade IV cancer**, with a focused and in-depth analysis of **glioblastoma**, the most aggressive and lethal primary brain tumor in adults.

The paper integrates findings from neuro-oncology, molecular biology, clinical medicine, cancer metabolism, and supportive care research. It is intended for **academic, educational, and research purposes** and is suitable for dissemination as a **preprint**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- To provide a structured overview of **Grade IV cancers**
- To analyze **glioblastoma classification, variants, and molecular biology**
- To summarize **clinical symptoms and diagnostic methods**
- To review **standard and emerging treatment strategies**
- To examine **treatment resistance mechanisms**
- To explore **dietary and lifestyle considerations**
- To highlight **ethical challenges and future research directions**

---

## 📌 Conclusion

Glioblastoma represents the most aggressive and lethal manifestation of Grade IV cancer, posing significant challenges in diagnosis, treatment, and long-term management. Despite advances in neurosurgery, radiotherapy, chemotherapy, and molecular profiling, patient outcomes remain poor due to tumor heterogeneity, invasive growth, and resistance to therapy. Emerging approaches such as immunotherapy, metabolic interventions, gene therapy, and precision medicine offer promising avenues but require further validation through large-scale clinical trials. A multidisciplinary and patient-centered approach—integrating medical treatment, supportive care, ethical considerations, and quality-of-life assessment—is essential. Continued global research collaboration remains critical for achieving meaningful therapeutic breakthroughs.
